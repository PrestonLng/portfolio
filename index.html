Top Ranked Ski Resorts

Mt. Bachelor, OR

Mount Bachelor Ski Resort in Oregon offers over 4,300 acres of terrain, 101 runs, and excellent snow conditions. It's a year-round destination for skiing, snowboarding, hiking, and mountain biking, with a vibrant base area for dining and shopping.

Beginner Friendliness: 7.9/10

Best Time of Year: December-March

Mt. Hood, OR

Mount Hood Meadows, located in Oregon, offers over 2,150 acres of skiable terrain with diverse runs for all levels. Known for its reliable snow and stunning mountain views, it provides skiing, snowboarding, and year-round outdoor activities.

Beginner Friendliness: 8.7/10

Best Time of Year: December-March

More Highly Ranked Ski Resorts

Mount Baker, WA

Mount Baker, in Washington, is known for its massive snowfall and rugged terrain. With over 1,000 acres of skiable slopes, it’s a favorite for advanced skiers and snowboarders. Its stunning alpine setting and laid-back atmosphere make it a unique Pacific Northwest destination.

Beginner Friendliness: 8.4/10

Best Time of Year: December-March

Buy

Mt. Crystal, WA

Mount Crystal, located in Washington, is a smaller ski area offering a more relaxed, family-friendly atmosphere. It features diverse terrain for skiing and snowboarding, with a focus on beginner and intermediate runs, and a cozy, less crowded experience.

Beginner Friendliness: 9.3/10

Best Time of Year: December-Febuary

Buy

Mt. Whistler Blackcomb

Whistler Blackcomb, in British Columbia, features over 8,100 acres of skiable terrain across two mountains. Known for its deep snow and diverse runs, it offers world-class skiing, snowboarding, and a lively village, making it a top year-round destination.

Beginner Friendliness: 7.8/10

Best Time of Year: December-Febuary

Buy
